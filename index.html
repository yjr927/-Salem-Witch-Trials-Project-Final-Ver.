The Salem Witch Trials, 1692

In 1692, fear spread through Salem Village, Massachusetts.

Several young girls began experiencing strange fits, screaming, shaking, and claiming that invisible forces were attacking them. In a deeply religious Puritan society, many villagers believed these events were caused by witchcraft.

The accusations quickly expanded. People were judged not only by evidence, but by rumor, reputation, gender expectations, poverty, and fear.

More than 200 people were accused. Nineteen people were executed by hanging, and one man, Giles Corey, was pressed to death.

This game places you inside three real historical cases from the Salem Witch Trials. Your role changes what you see, what power you hold, and how fear shapes your judgment.
